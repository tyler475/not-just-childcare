The Real Gap

Kids Ministry Is the Front Door. Most Churches Treat It Like a Closet.

Families decide whether they'll come back within the first 15 minutes of dropping their kids off. Before you preach a single word, the kids ministry has already won them or lost them. Yet most small churches run it as a babysitting service instead of front-line discipleship.

01

Understaffed and under-trained

The leader wasn't chosen, they were available. Volunteers are stretched thin and no one's been equipped for the role.

02

No systems that hold

Check-in, safety, and curriculum live in one person's head. When a volunteer calls out sick, the whole morning wobbles.

03

Families quietly leave

Without a safe, excellent, spiritually rich room, the families you worked so hard to reach drift away, and you never hear why.

Excellence isn't expensive. It's intentional.
